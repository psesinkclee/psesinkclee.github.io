See some recent things that I've worked on below and find me on LinkedIn, ResearchGate, Google Scholar, or Github.

OpenMaps

​

The City of Philadelphia releases a variety of open datasets on OpenDataPhilly, including a number that are spatially enabled.  OpenMaps was created using the Mapboard platform that was created in-house, to provide the public with a simple application to explore a selection of spatial city datasets.

 

 

StressIndex

​

One of my first big projects at the City of Philadelphia was the Stress Index.  By using my This is a storymap was created to display the results of summarizing different potential stressors down to residential parcels for the Community Schools Initiative.  This includes data from the American Community Survey, Philadelphia Police Department, the Center for Disease Control, and school districts to ultimately create a combined stress index used to identify priority schools across Philadelphia to act as neighborhood resource centers.

 

 

PHL Crime

​

This is a quick test of ESRI's dashboard theme using Philadelphia crime incidents data (available on OpenDataPhilly) that gets updated daily.  This data is also available on OpenMaps, but has added summary statistics and filters for the time incidents occurred (last 30, 14, 7, and 3 days) and for different categories of crimes.  All infographics update in real time while panning on the map to show information about crimes in the visible map extent.

 

 

Biomod2EZ

​

In graduate school, I focused on using distribution modeling algorithms to predict the occurrence of wildlife and wildlife infectious diseases across large geographical ranges under both current climate and future climate scenarios (see my publications here).  I wrote a suite of R scripts to use in conjunction with the biomod2 distribution modeling package to simplify it's use and generate a report with additional summary statistics and simple maps using Rmarkdown.  More info here.
